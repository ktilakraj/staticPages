Tennis for Beginners

Welcome to the world of tennis! Whether you’re new to the sport or just need a quick refresher, this guide will help you understand the basics of tennis so you can enjoy the game — and get the most out of your fantasy tennis experience.

The Basics of Tennis

Tennis is a racket sport played between two players (singles) or two teams of two players (doubles). The objective is simple: hit the ball over the net into your opponent’s court in a way that they cannot return it.

How the Game is Played

  • Serve: Each point starts with a serve. The server stands behind the baseline and hits the ball diagonally into the opponent’s service box.
  • Rally: After the serve, players hit the ball back and forth. The ball must bounce once or not at all on your side before you return it.
  • Scoring Points: You win a point when your opponent fails to return the ball within the rules.

How Tennis is Scored

Tennis scoring is unique but easy once you get the hang of it:

  • Game: A player must win 4 points to win a game, but must be ahead by 2 points. The points go like this:

    • 0 (Love) → 15 → 30 → 40 → Game

    If both players reach 40, it’s called Deuce. From there, a player must win two consecutive points to win the game.

  • Set: A player wins a set by winning 6 games, again with a margin of 2. If the set reaches 6–6, a tiebreak is usually played.
  • Match: Matches are usually best of 3 sets (or best of 5 in men’s Grand Slam matches).

Types of Tennis Tournaments

  • Grand Slams: The biggest and most prestigious tournaments — Australian Open, French Open (Roland Garros), Wimbledon, and US Open.
  • ATP/WTA Tours: Professional tennis circuits for men (ATP) and women (WTA). These include Masters 1000, 500, and 250-level tournaments.

Common Tennis Terms

  • Ace: A serve that the opponent can’t touch.
  • Double Fault: When a player misses both serves — results in losing a point.
  • Break Point: A chance for the receiving player to win the game.
  • Volley: Hitting the ball before it bounces.
  • Rally: A sequence of back-and-forth shots during a point.
